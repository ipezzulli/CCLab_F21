WHAT DO WE MEAN BY 'MATERIALS?'

×
In the textile industry, cloth is the collective term for fabrics and materials used in the production of clothing. Raw fibers go through a process of being twisted together to create threads. These threads are then woven or knit to create a workable material which can then be cut, stitched, and turned into garments.

Fibers can be either synthetic or natural. Each type of fiber is unique and bares different properties; some are sturdy and thick while others are smooth and flexible. Fibers are often combined together into blends giving a combination of desirable qualities.

The application of the clothing determines what type of fiber is used. For example a sports shirt would be better suited being made from a synthetic fiber with moisture wicking qualities, than from a material like wool which retains heat.
