NYLON

Nylon is a generic designation for a family of synthetic polymers. Nylon became important as a substitute for silk for the manufacture of parachutes during WWII at a time where silk had become a scarce commodity. Nylon was also used to make items like tents, ropes, and ponchos for the armed forces.


Common items used with this: Stretchy items such as swimwear, athleticwear, socks and stockings, and mesh fabric (such as the underlining of a dress)
